General description:
This book is a compilation of Ivy Global's four practice test booklets for the new SAT. It includes all four exams which can be used as diagnostic tests for personal study, as well as by organizations that run SAT courses and tutoring.
Why Ivy Global tests?
- Rigor-Tested Questions: Unlike other test products, we perform actual testing with high school students to help calibrate our test's difficulty.
- High-quality Passages: Passages for reading and essay sections are adapted from articles in the New York Times, the Atlantic, and other high-quality publications.
- Most like College Board: Our tests closely adhere to the College Board's test specifications and practice tests.
- Online Explanations: Thorough explanations of each question in our practice books.

Our experience:
There are lots of SAT practice tests out there, but a majority of them don’t reflect the style of the actual exam. We have gathered some of the best currently available. Generally, think of practice tests as tools for estimating your improvement and updating your work plan. Plenty of books we provided can serve as workbooks for different sections, skill sets, etc., but use practice tests from time to time in order to understand how your strengths and weaknesses evolved and what you have to work on. Always spread the practice tests out. There’s no point in taking all the tests at once. The tests should help you to see your progress; therefore, create a schedule and write them every once in a while. One more important detail is the timing. SAT is one of the most ruthlessly timed exams. Learning how to pace yourself properly is one of the most important skills; therefore, always use a timer when you write the practice tests. Don’t worry, you probably won’t be able to write everything within the allocated time at first. Don’t leave the questions unanswered or texts unread because of that. Write your first test with a stopwatch - see how long it takes you. But starting from the second one, time your practice tests and try to get quicker on each of them. Getting closer to your test date, write the practice tests more and more frequently. And most importantly, don’t forget to analyze your answers very carefully and learn the concepts you made mistakes on.

Note: If you have very limited time, focus only on practice tests. Analyze your results and try to learn the concepts you don’t know. The easiest way to boost your score is to learn grammar and math concepts.
