General description:
Intended to clearly and systematically demystify what is often considered the most challenging section of the SAT, The Critical Reader: The Complete Guide to SAT® Reading, 3rd Edition, provides a comprehensive review of the reading skills tested on the redesigned exam for students who are serious about raising their scores. Includes:

- A chapter-by-chapter breakdown of question types, with in-depth explanations and numerous examples demonstrating how to work through each type.
- Techniques for comprehending complex passages and identifying key information quickly and efficiently.
- Extensive strategies for simplifying and answering paired supporting evidence questions as well as graph- and chart-based data analysis questions.
- A list of alternate definitions of common words, plus strategies for using context clues to decipher the meaning of unfamiliar vocabulary.

To allow students to apply the strategies outlined in this book to College Board material while focusing on the specific areas in which they are seeking to improve, this book also includes a list of all the Reading questions from the Official College Board SAT Guide, 2018 Edition, arranged both by category and by test.

Our experience:
The Complete Guide to SAT Reading is a go-to tool for mastering skills for the reading section. This is a book by a remarkable author. Erica Meltzer is an experienced SAT tutor; all her books are very efficient. The Complete Guide to SAT Reading mainly focuses on offering useful strategies for the section. Generally, all prep books provide some kind of strategies, but in our experience, this one proved to be the best. Since reading is perceived as the hardest part of the exam, the book is definitely worth your time. It can boost your English section scores by up to 100(depending on when you take the book). In order to make the most of this guide, you may prefer to have some kind of understanding of the test structure beforehand. Therefore, we advise you to work on this book after finishing grammar and vocab books, as well as writing at least one full practice test. One more important note is that even though this guide includes exercises for the material it teaches you, we don’t think that’s enough. Make sure you write a lot of reading practice tests to properly use the strategies you learned - mastering only theoretical techniques without putting them into practice doesn’t do any good.
