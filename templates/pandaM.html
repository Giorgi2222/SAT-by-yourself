General description:
This book brings together everything you need to know for the SAT math section. Unlike most other test prep books, this one is truly geared towards the student aiming for the perfect score. It leaves no stones unturned. Inside, You'll Find:
- Clear explanations of the tested math concepts, from the simplest to the most obscure
- Hundreds of examples to illustrate all the question types and the different ways they can show up
- Over 500 practice questions and explanations to help you master each topic
- The most common mistakes students make (so you don't)

Our experience:
The College Panda's SAT Math is one of our favorite prep books. The College Panda guides are generally some of the best currently - they are specifically created for test-takers who aim at the perfect score. The math guide is designed for the third and fourth sections of the exam, and it can enable you to write those parts without a mistake. The book is structured around specific questions of SAT math, especially the ones that are most frequently mistaken, and teaches you how to solve them quickly. Even if you are very good at math, take into consideration that SAT is very limited in time. You may know how to solve particular problems mathematically but you may still want to consult the book to learn some tricks that help you answer entangled SAT questions in a more time-efficient manner. Since this book is a bit more advanced than foundational and contains only harder topics, you may want to work on it after you finish Math Workout for the SAT or Barron's Math Workbook. If you don’t have that much time or if you think you don’t need the lower-difficulty books, make sure you take at least one full practice test beforehand. Understanding the test structure is essential to make the most of the very specific approach The College Panda's Math Guide has.

Note: Consider complementing this book with The College Panda's 10 Practice Tests for the SAT Math (See description below).
